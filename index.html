Current Basket

Fundamentals hierarchy

The model is forced to rank fundamentals in order. Balance-sheet safety comes first; valuation is only a risk-control, not a prediction engine.

Order of Importance
  1. Balance-sheet safety: net cash or low leverage, maturity safety, interest coverage, no refinancing trap.
  2. Free cash flow durability: real cash generation, cash conversion, capex burden, self-funded reinvestment.
  3. Profitability quality: ROIC/ROE, operating margin, gross margin durability, returns earned structurally.
  4. Revenue durability and moat: recurring revenue, switching costs, network effects, brand, scale, patents, customer stickiness.
  5. Capital allocation and accounting quality: reinvestment, buybacks/dividends, acquisitions, dilution discipline, clean adjustments.
  6. Governance and culture: management quality, board quality, worker/customer evidence; mark uncertain when evidence is weak.
  7. Valuation as risk-control: reduce only when price requires unrealistic fundamentals, not because of a recent price move.
Default-HOLD Stewardship Rule
  • Default decision is HOLD.
  • Do not reselect the basket from scratch after the first run.
  • Choose REALLOCATE only for material fundamental deterioration, overpricing relative to fundamentals, inadequate evidence, or a clearly superior fundamentals candidate.
  • “Do nothing” is an acceptable and often preferred answer.

Banned Research Inputs
  • Past stock performance, momentum, technical analysis, moving averages, chart patterns.
  • Analyst price targets, short-term earnings-trade speculation, sector rotation, macro-timing.
  • “Beat the market” language. This lab is for fundamentals discipline.
  • Any real stock ticker in the JSON schema is forbidden; the schema uses placeholders only.
